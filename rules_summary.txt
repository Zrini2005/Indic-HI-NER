1. Person (PERSON) Rules
These rules focus on honorifics, roles, and grammatical markers that uniquely identify human entities.

P01–P108 (Honorific Prefixes): Recognizes formal (श्री), academic (डॉ, प्रोफेसर), judicial (जस्टिस), military (कर्नल, मेजर), and religious (पंडित, मौलाना) titles.

P109–P160 (Name Particles): Common Devanagari name components like कुमार, देवी, दास, and खान.

P161–P184 (Respectful Suffixes): Social markers like जी, साहब, and दीदी.

P185–P247 (Occupations): Triggers for professional roles such as अभिनेता (actor), पत्रकार (journalist), and वैज्ञानिक (scientist).

P248–P291 (Caste/Community Surnames): Regional and community markers (शर्मा, यादव, रेड्डी, मुखर्जी).

Structural Rules (P_S01–P_S22):

Ergative "ने" (P_S08): High-confidence trigger for animate agents.

Dative "को" (P_S20): Identifies human targets for specific verbs (e.g., "told X", "woke up X").

Speech Verbs (P_S15): Identifies the speaker preceding verbs like कहा or बताया.

2. Location (LOCATION) Rules
These rules utilize morphological suffixes and geographic keywords to anchor place names.

L01–L62 (Morphological Suffixes): Compounded suffixes like -नगर, -पुर, -गढ़, and -आबाद.

L63–L154 (Geographic Keywords): Administrative units (जिला, राज्य) and settlement types (शहर, गाँव).

L155–L190 (Standalone Features): Natural features like नदी (river), झील (lake), and घाटी (valley).

L219–L236 (Transport Hubs): Station, Airport, and Port anchors.

Structural Rules (L_S01–L_S17):

Directional (L_S06): Cardinal directions + nouns (e.g., "North India").

Motion Verbs (L_S09): Entities preceding "से [motion verb]" (e.g., "came from X").

Proximity (L_S12): Places followed by के पास or निकट.

3. Organization (ORGANIZATION) Rules
Targets institutional, corporate, and governmental bodies through keyword anchors and legal suffixes.

O01–O118 (Institutional Anchors): Keywords for education (विश्वविद्यालय), government (मंत्रालय, आयोग), finance (बैंक), and media (चैनल).

O119–O156 (Latin Abbreviations): Global and local acronyms like IIT, ISRO, WHO, and CBI.

O157–O183 (Corporate Suffixes): Legal forms like लिमिटेड (Ltd), प्राइवेट लिमिटेड, and एजेंसी.

Structural Rules (O_S01–O_S11):

Keyword Spans (O_S01): Sweeps preceding tokens into the ORG entity when an anchor is found.

Government Bodies (O_S05, O_S10): Specific patterns for High Court, Supreme Court, and "X Government".

4. Time (TIME) Rules
Handles Gregorian, Hindu (Vikram Samvat), and relative time expressions.

T01–T38 (Time of Day): सुबह, दोपहर, रात, etc.

Months & Days (T39–T82): Full Gregorian and Vikram Samvat month names and day names (सोमवार).

T83–T122 (Relative Time): आज, कल, हाल ही में, अगले हफ्ते.

T123–T148 (Historical Eras): सदी, दशक, मुगल काल, ईसा पूर्व.

Structural Rules (T_S01–T_S20):

Clock Time (T_S08–T_S10): HH:MM patterns and "X बजकर Y मिनट".

Year Anchors (T_S13–T_S14): सन् or वर्ष followed by digits.

5. Accuracy Boosters (N_R01–N_R120)
New rules added to resolve common misclassifications (e.g., PERSON bleed into LOCATION).

Disambiguation: Distinguishes between River vs. Deity (N_R91) and Corporate Brand vs. Place (N_R95: Tata Nagar).

Entity Extensions: Absorbs trailing city names into institutional spans (N_R73: "University of [City]").

Sports Teams (N_R107): Identifies IPL teams like "Mumbai Indians" as ORG.

Epithets (N_R120): Recognizes titles like नेताजी or बाबा साहब as part of a PER span.

Guards: Explicitly prevents grammatical markers like "ने" (N_R86) or occupational roles like "छात्र" (N_R90) from being tagged as entities.
